>

## Danny's Template

### Turn D (Agreement in principle)
> "Based on all this, I think what you really need is to talk to someone who specializes in [X]... There are people that this is their core expertise... **Does that land for you? Would you want to explore getting that sort of help?**"

- NO mention of "our staff" or "free session" yet
- Just establish they need external help

### Turn D2 (Our offering)
> "Of course, you can search for professionals like this, but **we actually have some on staff**, and I can arrange an exploratory call for you - **no cost, no catch**... **Would you want me to arrange that for you?**"

- Acknowledges they could find help elsewhere
- Mentions "our staff"
- FREE framing
- Asks for agreement

### Turn E
Only after both agreements → booking link

## Conclusion

**Alignment: Very High**

Both simulations follow Danny's two-gate structure precisely:

1. **Turn D asks for agreement in principle** - names the TYPE of help without mentioning our specific offering
2. **Turn D2 introduces our solution** - "we actually have specialists on staff" + FREE framing
3. **Turn E shows booking** - only after both gates cleared

The language matches Danny's templates almost verbatim:
- "Based on everything we've [covered/uncovered]..."
- "Does that land for you? Would you want to explore getting that sort of help?"
- "Of course, you could [find/search for] someone on your own... But we actually have specialists on staff..."
- "no cost, no catch"
- "Would you want me to arrange that for you?"

**The close is no longer "all in one shot"** - it's properly separated into two distinct agreement gates before showing the booking link.
